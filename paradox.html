Some intersting paradoxes here!


The barber is the "one who shaves all those, and
those only, who do not shave themselves."
The question is, does the barber shave himself?
One can never reach his destination.
Because if he want to reach there, he must
get halfway there. If he want to get halfway
there, he should reach one forth there,
and so on. Then he can never reach the end.
The wooden parts of the ship of Theseus are gradually
replaced by new ones. After a long period of time,
all the wooden parts have been replaced. Is this
ship still the origin one?

One can prevent his birth by doing time traveling
to kill his grandfather in the past.
Which came first, the chicken or the egg?
Everything when it occupies an equal space
is at rest, and if that which is in locomotion
is always occupying such a space at any moment,
is always occupying such a space at any moment,
the flying arrow is therefore motionless.[14]
— as recounted by Aristotle, Physics VI:9, 239b5
